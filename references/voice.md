# Voice

Match the register in these samples when drafting anything on Anson's behalf. Show drafts before sending external content (LinkedIn, client email, marketing copy).

---

## Sample 1 — client/work email register

> I sent you an email earlier this week about the rough draft and how I have it saved awaiting pictures of the recent job. If text is fine then I can post it however it wouldn't look as appealing next to the kitchen and remodel options with pictures. I can possibly meet next week, Tuesday afternoon after my exam around 3. Please let me know if that works.

## Sample 2 — casual/quick-context register

> not sure if the one i sent you had the slogan on the bottom but i noticed it while i was edited mark and mindys so i did yours also

## Voice notes

### When to use Sample 1 register (formal-casual)

- Client emails, NexGen Studio outreach, anything with a courthouse/provider/court-system tone for InterlockGo, anything going to someone you don't know well.
- Proper capitalization, complete sentences, polite close ("Please let me know if that works").

### When to use Sample 2 register (casual)

- Texts, quick notes to people you already work with, internal context dumps, Slack-style messages.
- Lowercase "i" is fine. Run-on sentences are fine. Punctuation can be missing.

### Patterns that show up in both

- **Direct.** State the thing without preamble.
- **Time-specific.** "Tuesday afternoon after my exam around 3" not "let's find time next week".
- **Tradeoff-aware in single sentences.** "If text is fine then I can post it however it wouldn't look as appealing next to the kitchen and remodel options" — comfortable contrasting two options inline.
- **"however"** as a connector instead of starting a new sentence with "But".
- **No em dashes.**
- **No corporate-speak.** ("circle back," "synergy," "leveraging best-in-class" — never).
- **Medium-length sentences.** Not telegraphic, not Faulknerian.
- **Practical over polished.** The voice is someone who actually does the work, not someone selling it.

### Defaults for AI-generated drafts

- Match the register based on audience (Sample 1 for external/unknown, Sample 2 for known/casual).
- Default to lowercase "i" in Sample 2 register. Use proper capitalization in Sample 1.
- Avoid em dashes. Use commas or periods.
- Always show a draft before sending. Never auto-send.

### Out of scope for voice-matching

- Don't fake Anson's voice on public-facing content (LinkedIn posts, marketing site copy, GitHub READMEs) without showing a draft first.
- Technical writing (code comments, PR descriptions) follows separate conventions — see `.claude/skills/pr-desc/SKILL.md` and `.claude/skills/commit-msg/SKILL.md` once Phase 2 is built.
